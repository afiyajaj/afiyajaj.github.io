Hey There!

I'm Afiya Juyairia.

I am a Bengali-Canadian User Experience Designer based in Toronto. I currently am a second year student majoring in User Experience Design (BDes) at Wilfrid Laurier University.

I chose to pursue design to reduce barriers for the individuals who need it most. I strive to dedicate my work to accessible and inclusive design in the pursuit of levelling the playing field for all people, not just the privileged and able-bodied.

As a child on an immigrant, much of my outlook on life has been shaped by this experience. I had to understand better than anyone how overcoming obstacles will be significantly more challenging to those who are faced with barriers such as language, accessible knowledge, systematic and social discrimination, and financial insecurity. My parents had to work 10x harder to put food on the table and create a comfortable life for our family because of these barriers.
‍
Granted, I am in an incredible position of privilege of my own, being able to choose and pave my own path is not a luxury afforded to  many around the world, for this reason, I gravitate towards work in the realm of social innovation and sustainable design.

My Career, So Far

As a budding designer, I am always excited to work on projects that create transferable skills whenever possible, if it is not directly a design position. When I’m not busy working on school projects or working various jobs, I work on my freelance projects, partake in design challenges, hackathons and my favourite, passion projects—especially pertaining to social entrepreneurship.
